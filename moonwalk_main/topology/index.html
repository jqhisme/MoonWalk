Topology of Storytellings

Have you ever tried to toggle the playback control of a film, trying to find a specific part you are looking for? If we consider the playback we can control as a linear projection of the story, the story itself may exist in much higher dimensions. There are so many possibilities of edit, dissemble, and reassemble a story, and these possibilities are perhaps lying and minging in a space that we might not be able to imagine. Moon Walk allows you to wander in this space using textual input, exploring can we get a vague understanding of the abstract information encoded in neural networks.

In this experiment, all films are being encoded into a coordinate with 1024 dimensions and reduced to 3 dimensions to be visualized on the screen. Every textual input will bring you to one of the coordinates, and a machine learning model will further bring you to one of the video clips in this space, disorienting and implicit, just like walking on the moon.
